# Five creative directions (full concept sheets)

> Raw research output from the 2026-07-04 concept workflow (14 agents). Preserved verbatim for implementation reference; not edited for style.

===== Digital Metabolism — vitals for the body you live inside a screen
NAMES: Vitals Terminal, Metabolic, Pulse & Ledger, Basal, Second Body
TAGLINE: Your other body has vitals too.
METAPHOR: The app treats your machine as a second body with its own physiology, and it reads that body's vitals instead of the computer's stats. Bytes are not numbers on a chart; they are metabolism. Every byte that moves through you is either fuel you take in or work you burn, and the app's whole job is to keep an honest daily energy balance between the two. Respiration is the network: bytes received are the inhale that brings the outside world in, and bytes sent are the exhale. Nutrition is tokens: the words and code an AI feeds you are calories with macros, protein when you generate your own thinking and simple carbs when you mostly consume completions. Storage is the disk: writes are what the body lays down as fat and muscle, the mass you carry forward, while reads are just the body drawing on reserves it already holds. The heartbeat is your hands: every keystroke is a beat and mouse travel is the distance that heart pushes blood. Screen time is simply the hours the body is awake. From these the app derives the vitals a real fitness tracker shows — a resting rate when you are idle, an exertion rate when you are working, an active-versus-rest ratio for the day — and closes each day like a metabolic panel: what you took in, what you burned, and whether you ended the day in surplus or in deficit.
WHY: The fit is honest because every one of the five families already behaves like a flow through a living system rather than a static count, and a body is precisely a machine for managing flows. Network traffic genuinely comes in bursts of in and out, which is what respiration is, so mapping received to inhale and sent to exhale describes the real shape of the data rather than decorating it. Tokens genuinely have a consumed-versus-generated split that maps cleanly onto intake versus output, and the protein-versus-carb reading is a truthful way to surface whether the user is doing their own thinking or leaning on the model. Disk writes really are the durable, cumulative residue of a day's activity, so calling them the mass the body stores is not a stretch; reads really are cheaper and transient, so treating them as drawing on reserves is accurate. Keystrokes arrive as a rate over time, exactly like a pulse, and a pulse is the single most legible vital a person already knows how to read. Screen time is literally waking hours. The metaphor also earns its keep by forcing a single unifying question — am I in surplus or deficit today — that a generic stats dashboard never asks, which is the difference between vitals and telemetry.
TONE: The voice is a calm, competent clinician who happens to like you: warm but never chirpy, precise but never cold. It states readings plainly, the way a good doctor reports a number without alarm or flattery, and it trusts you to draw your own conclusions. It never gamifies your body against you, never nags, and never moralizes about a high day or a low day, because a body in deficit is not a body that failed. Copy is short, declarative, and physical: "Resting. Pulse 12 bpm." or "Heavy intake today — mostly carbs." Humor is dry and rare, the aside of someone who has seen a thousand charts. When a metric is unavailable the tone stays unflustered, the way a nurse notes that one cuff is not attached rather than declaring an emergency.
VISUAL: The surface is a dark clinical instrument that reads like a modern vitals monitor at rest. The base is near-black charcoal in two steps, #0B0E11 for the window and #13181D for raised cards, so the app sits comfortably behind translucent macOS material and vibrancy. Signal color carries all meaning and nothing is colored for decoration. Respiration and the calm resting state use a cool oxygen teal #46E0C8; exertion, heartbeat, and surplus energy use a warm arterial coral #FF6B5B; nutrition and tokens use a nourishing amber #F5B546; storage and accumulated mass use a mineral violet #9B8CFF; deficit and depletion desaturate toward a slate #6B7686. A single hairline grid in #1E262D keeps the instrument feel. Typography pairs SF Pro Rounded for all the human-facing vitals and labels, because rounded numerals feel like a wearable rather than a server console, with SF Mono reserved strictly for raw byte counts and the ledger view where exactness is the point; the large hero readouts are SF Pro Rounded Semibold with tabular figures so digits never jitter as they tick. Motion is physiological, not decorative: the heartbeat pulse eases on a real cardiac curve with a sharp systole and a soft diastole rather than a symmetric sine, respiration rings breathe in and out on a slow four-second cycle, and everything settles with critically damped springs so nothing bounces like a toy. Texture is minimal — one faint scanline-free glow bloom behind an active vital so it looks lit from within, and thin ECG-style traces that draw left to right for pulse and respiration. The overall impression should be a bedside monitor designed by the people who make the Fitness rings, quiet until something is worth looking at.
FORM: The permanent home is a menubar extra showing one live vital at a time — by default the heartbeat as a tiny animated pulse trace with its current bpm — that the user can cycle through respiration, intake, and daily balance with a click, so the body is always glanceable without opening anything. Clicking opens a compact popover, the bedside monitor: three breathing rings (Intake, Output, Balance) over a live pulse and respiration trace, sized like the Now Playing popover and never larger. A separate full dashboard window, the Chart in the medical sense, is the deep view with the daily metabolic panel, the ledger, and history, opened only when the user wants to study rather than glance. Two desktop widgets ship in the small and medium sizes: a Pulse widget and a Daily Balance ring widget that live in Notification Center or on the desktop. A screensaver called Resting Rate turns the idle machine into a slow, beautiful full-screen vitals monitor of the day's traces, which doubles as the most honest ambient advertisement for what the app is. Everything below the full window degrades to whatever vitals are actually available.
VOCAB:
- AI usage: tokens consumed and generated -> Nutrition and Macros — the calories you eat | A single amber intake figure in kilocalorie-styled units (each 1,000 tokens reads as one digital Calorie) with a macro split bar underneath: generated tokens count as Protein, consumed completions as Carbs, so the bar shows at a glance whether the day was self-driven or model-fed. On the panel it appears as the day's meals stacked by tool (Claude Code, ChatGPT, local model), each meal a small amber block.
- Network activity: bytes sent and received -> Respiration — your breathing | A live teal respiration trace that inhales on bytes received and exhales on bytes sent, with a breaths-per-minute-style rate derived from burst frequency. The resting popover shows one slow breathing ring; the panel shows tidal volume for the day as total inhaled and exhaled, and flags apnea (a long silent stretch) or hyperventilation (a sustained flood) without judgment.
- Disk activity: bytes read and written -> Body Mass — what you store and what you spend | Writes register as mineral-violet Mass laid down, shown as a slowly filling reservoir that carries a cumulative all-time figure, since stored bytes are the body you build over time. Reads show as a lighter draw-down pulse against that reservoir. The panel separates Laid Down (writes) from Drawn (reads) so the durable residue of the day is distinct from mere activity.
- Screen time: awake-display time -> Waking Hours — time the body is awake | The clock that scales every rate into a per-hour vital and anchors the day. Shown as a thin ring around the whole monitor that fills across waking hours, dimming during sleep and lighting during use, so every other vital is implicitly read against how long the body was actually up. The panel reports Awake, Longest Session, and Rest.
- Input: keystrokes and mouse travel -> Pulse and Circulation — your heartbeat | Keystrokes are the heartbeat, drawn as a coral ECG-style pulse trace with a live bpm (beats per minute) that rises during focused work and falls to a resting rate when idle — the app's signature number. Mouse travel is Circulation, the distance that pulse pushes, shown as a cumulative kilometers-traveled figure the way a watch shows steps. Resting Pulse over the trailing week becomes a real baseline vital you can watch trend.
SIGNATURE: The Daily Metabolic Panel with a true energy balance. At the end of each day the app closes the body's books the way a fitness ring closes, but instead of one goal it computes a single honest balance between everything taken in (bytes received, tokens consumed, reads drawn from reserves) and everything burned or laid down (bytes sent, tokens generated, keystrokes beaten, writes stored, distance circulated), and tells you whether the day ended in surplus or deficit — a body that consumed more than it produced, or produced more than it consumed. This is the wow because no system monitor asks that question, and the answer is genuinely legible about your day: a heavy-reading, heavy-scrolling, low-output day reads as surplus and sedentary, while a day of hard original writing and shipping reads as high exertion and deficit. Over weeks it produces a resting baseline and a trend line for your digital metabolism, so you can see your second body's fitness the way a watch shows your first.
RITUALS: The daily ritual is Closing the Panel: shortly after your last waking hour the menubar pulse slows to your resting rate and a quiet card offers the day's metabolic panel — intake, output, balance, and one plain-language line such as "Deficit day. High exertion, light intake." No streaks, no guilt, just a reading you can dismiss with a glance. The weekly ritual is the Resting Rate report every Sunday evening, which reports your resting pulse, average breaths, and total mass laid down for the week against your trailing baseline, the way a wearable summarizes your week. The yearly ritual is the Annual Physical, a single scrollable full-window recap that reads like a clinician's year-end chart: your busiest metabolic day, your longest apnea, total distance circulated, total mass now carried, and your metabolism's trend across twelve months — celebratory but clinical, never a leaderboard.
PRIVACY: The stance is counts, never contents, and it is stated in exactly those words on first launch and in the menubar's About line. The app measures the body's vitals, not its thoughts: it counts that a key was struck, never which key; it measures how many bytes breathed in and out, never what they carried; it tallies tokens by volume and by tool, never a word of any prompt or completion. Everything is computed and stored locally in a single on-device store, nothing leaves the machine, there is no account, no server, and no analytics, and the app ships with no network client of its own so it has nothing to phone home with — a claim any user can verify with Little Snitch or the macOS firewall, which the app openly invites. AI token figures come only from local logs already on disk, such as Claude Code's own session files, so reading nutrition never requires touching a cloud. A one-switch Blackout instantly pauses all sensing and shows a clear indicator, because a body deserves to close the blinds.
V1 CUTS: Version one deliberately ships no cloud, no account, no sync across machines, and no companion iOS app, because a body is one machine and multi-device physiology can wait. It sets no goals, awards no streaks, and delivers no coaching or nudges, since the founding promise is a mirror and not a nag, and gamified goals would betray the clinical tone. It attempts no per-app or per-website attribution of traffic, which keeps the design firmly on counts-not-contents and sidesteps a swamp of parsing. It supports only the AI tools whose token counts can be read from local logs on day one, presenting others as unattached cuffs rather than faking data. It omits data export and a public API for now, ships a single built-in visual theme rather than customization, and leaves the screensaver's interactivity minimal. The point of these cuts is to protect the two things that make the direction work: an honest privacy posture and a calm, non-judgmental voice.
RISK: The biggest risk is that the physiology metaphor becomes a costume the data cannot honestly wear, so the app feels like a themed stats monitor rather than true vitals — the failure mode is a user who reads "you are in metabolic deficit" and cannot tell what it means about their actual day, at which point the poetry curdles into noise. This is sharpest for the energy-balance formula, which must be tuned so its surplus and deficit readings track something a person recognizes about how they spent their hours; if the balance feels arbitrary, the signature feature collapses and takes the concept with it. The secondary risk is graceful degradation under missing metrics: if half the vitals are unattached cuffs on a fresh install (especially AI tokens, which may only come from Claude Code logs at first), the bedside monitor can look broken rather than partial, so the empty states must feel like a body at rest with some sensors off, not a dashboard with holes. Both risks are about credibility, and the direction lives or dies on whether the vitals feel true.

===== The Instrument — a precision bench meter for your digital life
NAMES: Rheo, GAUGE//1, Aperture Meter, Datum, Fathom
TAGLINE: Read your life in bytes.
METAPHOR: The app is a single, beautifully machined bench instrument that you keep on your desk, the way an audio engineer keeps a VU meter bridge or a photographer keeps a light meter. It does not advise, gamify, or nag. It measures. Every metric family is a calibrated channel on the same instrument, each with its own needle, scale, and unit, all reading from one honest signal path. The emotion is the quiet confidence of holding a well-made tool: the satisfaction of a needle that sits exactly where it should, of a scale you trust, of numbers that are the hero because the instrument never lies to make itself look interesting.
WHY: Bytes are already a physical measurement, so treating them like current, sound pressure, or luminance is not a costume, it is honest. A byte per second is a rate exactly like amperes or decibels, which means a needle deflecting across a calibrated scale is the truthful native representation, not a decoration. The instrument metaphor also forces the design toward accuracy rather than persuasion, and an app that counts your keystrokes needs to feel like a measuring device you trust rather than a coach that judges you. Because a real instrument is proud of its precision and its limits, the metaphor gives us a graceful, in-character way to show a channel as uncalibrated when a data source such as AI tokens is not yet wired in, instead of hiding the gap.
TONE: Restrained, exact, and quietly proud, the way good lab equipment is proud. The app speaks in units and readings, never in praise or scolding. It reports what it measures with the calm authority of a tool that has been calibrated, and it never editorializes about whether a number is good or bad. Warmth comes entirely from craft, from the feel of the needles and the honesty of the scales, not from personality or copy.
VISUAL: The instrument reads as a machined aluminum face on near-black, using a warm off-white dial (#F4F1EA) and a graphite chassis (#1A1B1D) with a brushed-metal bezel rendered as a subtle vertical gradient from #3A3C40 to #202225. Scale markings, tick marks, and numerals are hairline (0.5pt at 2x) in ink black (#141414) on the light dial and in #C9C6BE on dark channels. One reserved accent, a signal amber (#E8A317), lights only live activity and the redline zone, and a single cold cyan (#4FB6C4) marks the calibration and peak-hold marks. Type is uncompromising: dial numerals and all readouts are set in a monospaced grotesque such as Berkeley Mono or, as a licensed fallback, IBM Plex Mono, so digits align in perfect columns and never jitter as they update; small caps labels and unit annotations use Söhne or Neue Haas Grotesk at tight tracking for the Braun-era engineering feel. Needles are true anti-aliased vectors with a faint drop shadow and physically modeled motion using a lightly damped spring, so they overshoot a hair and settle, exactly like a moving-coil meter, and they smear into a soft motion blur on fast transients. Texture is minimal and tactile: a barely-there radial paper grain on the dial, screw-head details at the bezel corners, and an engraved serial number that is really the app version. Everything is flat, square-shouldered, and generously margined, with no gradients on data and no gloss, honoring Rams while the amber accent and the exposed calibration controls bring the Teenage Engineering wink.
FORM: The primary form is a menubar extra that is itself a tiny live meter: a compact horizontal bar showing the single most active channel as a moving amber segment bar with its numeric readout, so the instrument is always glanceable in the menubar without opening anything. Clicking it drops down the Meter Bridge, a fixed-size main window laid out as one instrument panel with all five channels stacked as horizontal VU-style meters plus a large primary analog dial at top that you assign to any channel. A separate Desktop Panel is offered as a set of macOS desktop widgets in three sizes, where the small widget is one round gauge, the medium is a two-channel meter strip, and the large is a mini meter bridge. A Bench Mode screensaver turns the whole display into a slow, cinematic instrument panel with peak-hold trails for ambient viewing. The window chrome is deliberately minimal and non-resizable so the instrument always keeps its exact proportions, the way real hardware has a fixed faceplate.
VOCAB:
- AI usage (tokens consumed and generated) -> COGNITION channel, read in tokens per minute with a running total | A dedicated VU meter labeled COGNITION where the needle tracks live token throughput in tok/min, with two hairline sub-scales for IN (consumed) and OUT (generated). A small amber peak-hold pip marks the session's busiest burst. When only Claude Code logs are available and other tools are not wired in, the scale shows an engraved UNCALIBRATED — LOCAL SRC tag and the needle still moves for the sources it has, so the channel degrades honestly rather than reading zero.
- Network activity (bytes sent and received) -> TRAFFIC channel, read in bytes per second down and up | A twin-needle meter with a down needle and an up needle sharing one logarithmic B/s scale from bytes to gigabytes, so both a trickle and a saturated link sit legibly on the same face. The needles twitch continuously with live throughput and smear on spikes, and a cyan cumulative odometer beneath the dial counts total bytes moved today.
- Disk activity (bytes read and written) -> STORAGE channel, read in bytes per second read and write | A horizontal segmented bar meter, styled like a mixing-console fader scale, with read filling leftward and write filling rightward from center, each segment lit in amber as I/O rises into a redline zone. A peak-hold cap freezes the highest recent segment for a few seconds so bursts remain readable after they pass.
- Screen time (time in front of an awake display) -> EXPOSURE channel, read as an accumulating elapsed dial | A round chronometric dial where a slow-sweeping hand accumulates awake-display time for the day, marked in hours, with a thin outer ring that fills across a 24-hour reference so the day's exposure is a single arc. Unlike the fast channels this needle moves imperceptibly, deliberately contrasting slow time against fast bytes, and it parks when the display sleeps.
- Input (keystroke count and mouse travel) -> MECHANICS channel, read in keystrokes per minute and cumulative travel | A dual readout showing a KPM needle for live typing cadence and a separate distance odometer for mouse and trackpad travel converted into real units such as meters, so the day's pointer motion reads like the trip meter on a machined instrument. Only counts and distances are ever shown, never characters or positions, and the odometer rolls with a mechanical digit animation.
SIGNATURE: Calibration and Peak-Hold. Every channel has a real, tactile calibration control, a knurled rotary knob you can turn to set the meter's full-scale range for your own machine, exactly like adjusting the sensitivity on a bench instrument, so a heavy user and a light user both get needles that live in the expressive middle of the scale rather than pinned or dead. Paired with it is instrument-grade Peak-Hold: each meter drops a glowing marker at its highest reading and holds it, and a single Recalibrate gesture sweeps all needles to full scale and back in a satisfying self-test animation, the same way pro audio and aviation gear does a startup needle sweep to prove every channel is alive. This one interaction turns passive stats into a tool you actually tune, and it is the wow moment that no generic dashboard has.
RITUALS: Daily, the instrument performs a startup Self-Test at first login, sweeping all needles to full scale and back so you see every channel confirm it is live, then it settles into the day's live reading. At end of day it prints a Daily Log Card, a single receipt-styled panel with the day's totals and peaks in monospaced columns, quietly noting which channel redlined. Weekly, on a day you choose, it issues a Calibration Report that overlays the week's seven daily arcs so you can see the rhythm of your digital life as stacked traces, and it suggests updated full-scale settings if your usage has drifted. Yearly, it renders The Annual Readout, a printable large-format instrument panel poster of the year's cumulative totals across all five channels, the digital-life equivalent of an odometer photo at a milestone, designed to be beautiful enough to actually hang.
PRIVACY: The instrument measures signals, never contents, and it says so plainly on its own face. It counts keystrokes but never records which keys, it measures mouse travel but never where the pointer went, and it reads network and disk throughput but never inspects a single packet or file. Every reading is computed and stored locally on the Mac, with no account, no server, and no telemetry leaving the device, which is enforced by shipping with no networking entitlement at all so the app is technically incapable of phoning home. A visible Signal Path panel documents exactly which macOS API feeds each channel and what it can and cannot see, and the accessibility and input-monitoring permissions the keystroke and mouse channels require are requested transparently with a plain explanation that only aggregate counts are derived. The honest core promise is that this is a meter, and a meter reads magnitudes, not meanings.
V1 CUTS: Version one deliberately ships as a read-only instrument and nothing more. It leaves out all goals, streaks, alerts, and gamification, because a meter that scolds you is no longer a meter. It leaves out cloud sync, multi-device aggregation, and any account system, keeping everything on one Mac. It leaves out historical analytics beyond the day, the week overlay, and the yearly poster, so there are no deep trend graphs, no CSV data-mining views, and no cross-metric correlations in v1. It also leaves out broad AI-tool coverage, launching with local Claude Code logs as the only calibrated Cognition source and honestly marking the rest as uncalibrated until real connectors are built. Custom themes, iOS companions, and any Windows or web version are all out of scope for the native-macOS v1.
RISK: The biggest risk is that the instrument is exquisite but inert, that people admire the needles for a week and then stop opening it because a beautiful meter with no interpretation gives them nothing to do. The metaphor's discipline, refusing to advise or gamify, is also its commercial vulnerability, since the ambient menubar meter must be genuinely glanceable and the daily and yearly rituals must carry enough meaning to earn a permanent place on the desk. A close second risk is permissions friction and trust, because asking for input monitoring to count keystrokes is exactly the request a privacy-conscious Mac user is most trained to reject, so the honest local-first stance has to be communicated flawlessly at the very first launch or the instrument never gets to take its first reading.

===== Symbiont — a single-celled digital lifeform that metabolizes your bytes
NAMES: Symbiont, Chondria, Endosymbiont, Culture, Petri
TAGLINE: Your bytes, alive.
METAPHOR: A single microorganism lives in the menubar, rendered as a soft translucent cell with a visible interior. It is explicitly framed as a symbiont: a tiny lifeform that survives entirely by eating the byte-exhaust of your digital day. Every metric family is a different nutrient it ingests through a dedicated organelle, and because the five nutrients are metabolized by different internal machinery, a heavy AI day and a heavy typing day produce visibly different bodies. AI tokens are protein that build new internal structures. Network bytes are water and dissolved minerals that swell or shrink its volume. Disk bytes are dense carbohydrate stored as granules. Screen-time is sunlight that a chloroplast turns into a green glow. Input is kinetic food that grows and beats the cilia lining its membrane. The cell never dies and never scolds, because a cell has no opinion about what it eats; it simply becomes the shape of its diet. Over days it molts, divides, and shifts color, so the menubar shows you the literal metabolic portrait of how you spent your digital life.
WHY: The metaphor fits byte data honestly because metabolism is the one biological process that is genuinely and only about throughput, which is exactly what every one of these five metrics measures. A cell does not care whether the bytes were good or bad, productive or wasted; it only registers how much of each nutrient arrived and converts mass into form. That maps one-to-one onto the founder's premise that these are neutral volume counters, not moral scores. The five metric families also map cleanly onto five real, distinct cellular subsystems (protein synthesis, osmotic water balance, energy-storage granules, photosynthesis, and motility), which means the design earns the promise that a heavy AI day feeds it differently than a heavy typing day rather than just recoloring the same blob. Crucially, a cell has no face, so it cannot frown, which delivers the no-guilt requirement structurally rather than as a rule we have to remember to enforce.
TONE: The tone is calm, curious, and quietly scientific, closer to a naturalist's field notebook than to a game. Copy speaks the way a gentle microbiologist narrates a specimen under glass: observational, never congratulatory and never disappointed. It states what the organism is doing and lets you draw conclusions. The register is warm but restrained, with dry wit reserved for edge cases, for example when a metric source goes dark the app says the corresponding organelle has gone dormant rather than raising an alarm.
VISUAL: The organism is rendered in SwiftUI with Metal-backed soft-body shaders so the membrane wobbles and the interior granules drift with real fluid motion. The palette is a wet-lab microscopy palette on a near-black field: the base membrane is a translucent cyan-white (#DCEFF3) over a background of #0B0F12, with each nutrient owning one accent so a glance reads the day's diet by color. AI protein is amber (#E0A44B), network water is a cool aqua (#4FB0C6), disk carbohydrate granules are ochre (#C77D3A), screen-time photosynthesis is a chlorophyll green (#5FC46A), and input motility renders as magenta cilia (#D8567F). Motion is slow and living, with a resting membrane pulse near one beat per second that speeds up with input volume, so the creature visibly breathes faster on a busy day. Typography is SF Pro Rounded for the organism's ambient labels and gentle counters, giving a soft biological feel, paired with SF Mono for exact byte figures so the real numbers read as instrument data. The main window frames the cell like a slide under a microscope, complete with a faint circular vignette and a thin scale bar, and the overall texture goal is bioluminescent glass, luminous and semi-transparent, never flat or cartoonish.
FORM: The primary surface is a menubar extra showing the living cell at 22 points, animated and legible at a glance. Clicking it opens a popover that is the microscope slide: the organism enlarged, five nutrient readouts arranged around it, and a today-versus-usual line. A separate main dashboard window ("the Slide") offers the full lab view with per-metric history, the growth timeline, and the molt and division log. Two desktop widgets ship in v1, a small square widget showing just the cell and a medium widget adding the five nutrient bars. A screensaver called Culture shows the colony across recent days drifting in a dark medium, each past self a faded cell, turning your digital history into an aquarium. Everything degrades to the menubar creature alone if the user wants nothing else on screen.
VOCAB:
- AI usage (tokens consumed and generated) -> Protein synthesis | Amber protein bodies accumulate inside the cell and assemble into visible internal structures such as a denser nucleus and new organelles, so a heavy AI day makes the creature structurally more complex and organized. The popover shows exact tokens in and out in SF Mono, split by tool (Claude Code, ChatGPT, local models), and gracefully collapses to a single 'local logs only' protein source when other sources are unavailable, dimming that organelle rather than hiding it.
- Network activity (bytes sent and received) -> Osmosis and hydration | Aqua fluid governs the cell's turgor and overall size: bytes received swell it plump and round, bytes sent contract and streamline it. The readout gives precise sent and received totals, and the membrane visibly tightens or balloons in near real time so a big download literally inflates the organism.
- Disk activity (bytes read and written) -> Carbohydrate granules (energy stores) | Ochre storage granules settle toward the base of the cell, packing denser as writes accumulate and clearing slightly as reads draw them down. The count of granules maps to gigabytes, and hovering reveals exact read and write byte totals; a disk-heavy day gives the creature a heavy, sediment-rich lower body.
- Screen time (time before an awake display) -> Photosynthesis (sunlight) | A chlorophyll-green chloroplast brightens with accumulated awake-display minutes, casting a green internal glow that intensifies through the day and fades overnight. The exact hours and minutes appear beside a small sun icon, and long unbroken sessions produce a saturated, almost sunburned green that reads as a lot of light without any nagging language.
- Input (keystrokes and mouse travel) -> Motility (cilia and heartbeat) | Magenta cilia line the membrane; keystroke volume grows their number and length while cumulative mouse travel sets how fast they wave, and total input drives the membrane's pulse rate so a frantic day makes the whole creature beat faster. The readout shows keystroke count and mouse distance converted to a human scale such as meters or marathons, always as counts of events and never as captured content.
SIGNATURE: The signature feature is Division: when the organism's total accumulated bytes cross a threshold, it undergoes mitosis and splits into two, and the parent cell becomes a permanent, explorable specimen in your Culture history, a fixed portrait of exactly what your digital metabolism looked like during that stretch of life. Scrolling back through the colony is like flipping through a photo album made entirely of your own byte-diet, where you can see the season you lived inside Claude Code (amber and structured), the week you downloaded everything (swollen and aqua), or the sprint you typed through (fast cilia, racing pulse). It turns abstract lifetime totals into a lineage of little creatures you can revisit, which no bar chart can do.
RITUALS: The daily ritual is a quiet dusk feeding summary: once in the evening the popover offers a one-line note on what the cell ate most today, for example that today was a protein-rich day, with the numbers a click away and no streak or score attached. The weekly ritual is the Molt, where each week the organism sheds a thin translucent husk that is saved as that week's silhouette, giving a scannable strip of seven-day shapes to compare weeks at a glance. The yearly ritual is the Culture Report, an animated slow drift through the full year's colony that plays like a time-lapse of every cell you grew, ending on cumulative lifetime totals rendered as the size and color your organism reached, presented as a keepsake rather than a performance review.
PRIVACY: The stance is local-first and counts-not-contents, stated plainly and enforced structurally. The app records how many keystrokes occurred and how far the mouse traveled, never which keys or where, and it reads AI token counts from local logs such as Claude Code's without transmitting prompts or completions anywhere. All metabolism is computed on-device and stored in a local database inside the app's sandbox container, with no account, no server, and no telemetry in v1; the organism lives entirely on your Mac. A one-screen Membrane panel shows exactly which macOS permissions are active (input monitoring, network stats, disk stats, display state) and lets you starve any single organelle by revoking its nutrient, in which case that subsystem visibly goes dormant and the rest of the creature carries on, so privacy trade-offs are legible in the organism itself rather than buried in settings.
V1 CUTS: Version 1 deliberately leaves out any cloud sync, account system, or cross-device colony, since a symbiont that lives on one Mac is the honest and shippable scope. It cuts social features entirely, meaning no sharing, leaderboards, comparison to other users, or friend colonies, because comparison reintroduces exactly the guilt the direction refuses. It ships with only local Claude Code logs as the guaranteed AI protein source and treats ChatGPT and other tools as optional organelles that light up if and when a local source exists, rather than promising integrations that require sending data out. It also holds back user-authored goals, reminders, and any notification that could read as nagging, plus deeper species customization such as choosing your organism's body plan, deferring those to a later release so v1 stays a calm, self-contained specimen.
RISK: The biggest risk is that the metaphor becomes decorative rather than legible, meaning users enjoy the pretty wobbling cell but cannot actually tell what a given shape means about their day, which would reduce it to an aquarium screensaver with no dashboard value. The mapping from five nutrients to visible body changes has to be distinct and learnable enough that a returning user reads the creature the way they would read a chart, otherwise the real numbers behind the click become the only thing anyone uses and the organism is just skin. Mitigating this requires disciplined visual encoding, a short first-run explainer that names each organelle once, and an always-available toggle to the plain readouts, so the biology stays an accurate instrument and never drifts into empty cuteness.

===== The Byte Almanac — a daily typeset broadsheet of your digital life
NAMES: Broadsheet, The Daily Byte, Almanac (by ByteLife), Foolscap, Colophon
TAGLINE: Your year, set in type.
METAPHOR: The app is a private printing press that sets one page of type per day. Every metric family becomes a department of an old newspaper-cum-almanac: your machine has weather (network traffic), tides (token flow), a farmer's yield (disk read and write), daylight hours (screen time), and a labor column (keystrokes and mouse mileage). At dawn the press composes the previous day's page, prints a single automatically written observation as the day's headline dispatch, and files the page into the running volume. The metaphor treats data not as a live gauge to stare at but as a record to be read after the fact, the way you read a morning paper about a day that already happened. At year's end the twelve months bind into one volume, so the emotional arc of the product is not monitoring but keeping and eventually rereading a diary written in bytes.
WHY: Byte counters are cumulative and retrospective by nature. You cannot act on the exact number of bytes your disk wrote at 3pm, and a live-updating gauge of it is meaningless anxiety. What byte data actually is is a record, a tally that only becomes interesting once a period closes and you can compare today against yesterday or this month against last. The almanac form is built for exactly that shape of data: it is periodic, it summarizes a closed interval, and it pairs raw figures with a short human reading of them. An almanac has always mixed hard numbers such as tide tables and sunrise times with plain-language observation, which is precisely the pairing this app needs between a token count and a sentence about it. The five families also map cleanly and honestly onto almanac departments without forcing, because each family is a flow measured over a day, and a day is the almanac's natural unit.
TONE: Calm, literate, and quietly witty in the manner of a good almanac editor. The voice never hypes, never gamifies, and never scolds you for screen time. It observes and records. It is warm but understated, the tone of a well-set page that trusts the reader to draw conclusions. Occasional dry humor appears in the composed observations, but the register stays reflective and adult, closer to a field notebook or a printed diary than to a fitness tracker.
VISUAL: The whole app is designed as print on paper rather than glass. The page background is a warm off-white paper stock at roughly #F4F0E6 in light mode, with a near-black ink at #1C1B18 for body type, and it inverts to a dark letterpress mode with warm cream ink #E8E2D2 on a deep ink ground #17161380. One restrained accent, a rubricated red at #9E2B25 borrowed from illuminated almanacs, marks headlines, drop caps, and the single daily observation. Typography carries the identity and uses only real, license-clean faces. Headlines and the masthead are set in a high-contrast Scotch or Didone display face such as Playfair Display for a broadsheet feel. Body figures, tables, and column text are set in a robust text serif such as Source Serif 4 or the free Libre Caslon Text for the almanac register. All numerals, tide tables, and byte counts are set in a monospaced slab such as IBM Plex Mono so columns of figures align on the decimal like a real ledger. Rules are hairline at 0.5pt, columns are separated by thin vertical rules, and a woodcut-style masthead ornament sits at the top of each day's page. Motion is deliberately minimal and paper-like: pages do not animate live, they turn. Turning to a new day uses a slow one-shot page-curl or a fade-through-paper transition of about 400ms with an ease-out curve, and numbers set once when a page is composed rather than ticking upward. Texture is a very subtle paper grain and a faint letterpress deboss on headlines, kept light enough to stay legible on a Retina display.
FORM: The center of gravity is a main dashboard window shaped like a single broadsheet page, one day per page, with a spine of month tabs down the side and a page-turn gesture between days. A menubar extra sits alongside it as the press's ticker: a small mono glyph and one live figure of your choosing (for example today's tokens or today's daylight hours), with a dropdown showing today's page in miniature. A macOS desktop widget in the small and medium sizes renders the current day's masthead plus the one composed observation, so the almanac's voice is visible without opening the app. A screensaver and dynamic wallpaper mode display the current page as a framed broadsheet on the desk, which is where the year-end bound volume also lives as a slow page-turning presentation.
VOCAB:
- AI usage (tokens consumed and generated) -> The Tides | A tide table in the almanac tradition, set in monospaced figures with two columns headed 'flood' for tokens received from models and 'ebb' for tokens you sent, per tool. A small hand-inked tide curve runs above the table showing the day's high-water mark, and the tool with the highest flood is named in the day's dispatch.
- Network activity (bytes sent and received) -> The Weather | The day's weather report, rendered as a woodcut sky glyph (clear, overcast, storm) chosen by traffic volume, with 'received' shown as precipitation and 'sent' as wind, each given in human-readable bytes. A one-line forecast-style sentence sits beneath, for example 'Heavy inbound weather after noon, clearing by evening.'
- Disk activity (bytes read and written) -> The Yield | A farmer's-almanac harvest column with two figures, 'reaped' for bytes read and 'sown' for bytes written, set beside a small sheaf woodcut whose fullness scales with the day's total. Written across the running week it forms a planting-and-harvest strip so heavy write days read as sowing seasons.
- Screen time (time before an awake display) -> Daylight | A sunrise-to-sunset daylight bar, the almanac's hours-of-daylight entry, showing when the display was lit across the 24-hour clock as a warm band with dark gaps for time away. The total is given as 'hours of daylight' and the longest unbroken stretch is noted as the day's 'solstice'.
- Input (keystrokes and mouse travel) -> The Labor Column | A labor-and-toil ledger set in the ledger monospace, with keystrokes given as a plain count and mouse travel converted to real distance and rendered in a period unit such as furlongs or miles walked by the cursor. A small margin note translates the distance into something legible, for example 'the cursor walked 1.2 miles today, about the length of the pier.'
SIGNATURE: The Dispatch: a single automatically composed one-line observation printed as each day's headline, written by a small local template-and-language engine that reads the day's figures and finds the one true, non-obvious thing to say about them. It never merely restates a number. It notices relationships and records them in the almanac's own dry voice, for example 'You generated more words with machines than you typed by hand for the first time this week,' or 'A quiet day at the press: least network weather since the 3rd, and the cursor barely left its chair.' The Dispatch is what turns a stats readout into something a person actually wants to reread, and at year's end the 365 dispatches compile into the front matter of the bound volume as a chronicle of your digital year in sentences.
RITUALS: The daily ritual is the morning edition: at a time you set, the press composes yesterday's page and quietly files it, so opening the app in the morning means reading about the day that just ended rather than watching today tick. The weekly ritual is the Sunday supplement, a folded double-page spread that sets the week's seven pages side by side with one composed weekly dispatch and the week's high-water and low-water marks. The yearly ritual is the binding: on the last days of the year the app offers to bind your 2026 into a single volume with a masthead, a title page, twelve monthly section pages, the compiled dispatches as front matter, and a colophon recording totals, which the user can page through as a screensaver, export as a print-ready PDF, and keep.
PRIVACY: The stance is local-first and counts-not-contents, stated plainly on the colophon page of every volume so it travels with the record. The app records only tallies: how many keystrokes, never which keys, and how many bytes, never which bytes or to what destination. Nothing is uploaded, there is no account, and there is no network call the app makes on your behalf beyond reading local counters, which the colophon attests to. All data lives in a local store the user owns, the year-end volume is generated on-device, and the honest framing is that this is a press with no wire service: it prints only what your own machine already knows about itself, and it prints it only for you.
V1 CUTS: Version one deliberately leaves out live real-time dashboards and second-by-second gauges, because the whole premise is retrospective daily composition rather than monitoring. It ships without cloud sync, sharing, social comparison, and leaderboards, since the volume is a private diary and not a feed. It cuts configurable custom widgets and a plugin system for arbitrary data sources, and it does not attempt goals, streaks, or nudges, because the almanac observes rather than coaches. AI-token capture in version one reads only from local Claude Code logs and any other locally available agent logs, and every other AI tool is simply left as a blank column in the tide table until a local source exists, which the design already accommodates.
RISK: The biggest risk is that the composed Dispatch is the entire soul of the product and also the hardest thing to get right. If the daily observation reads as a generic Mad-Libs template ('You used 42,000 tokens today!') rather than a genuine, surprising, well-phrased reading, the whole editorial premise collapses into a stats dashboard wearing a serif costume, and the user stops opening it after a week. The engine must reliably find the one non-obvious relationship in each day's figures and phrase it in a consistent voice, entirely on-device with no cloud model in version one, across days that are often boringly similar. A secondary risk is that graceful degradation of missing metrics, such as tokens from tools with no local log, could leave the page looking half-empty, so the layout must make an absent department read as an intentional blank column in the almanac rather than as a broken feature.

===== Double-Entry Self: The Ledger of Your Digital Estate
NAMES: Reconcile, Bytekeeping, The Standing Ledger, Nightaudit, Ampersand & Co.
TAGLINE: Balance the books on your digital life.
METAPHOR: The app treats your digital life as a business you unknowingly incorporated, and it installs you as the sole accountant of that estate. Every byte is a transaction that must post to a book: bytes and tokens flowing outward are credits, bytes and attention flowing inward are debits, and the two columns are meant to balance the way a real ledger does. The day is an accounting period that opens when your display first wakes and closes when you run the nightly reconciliation, and the app's dry, deadpan voice is that of a bookkeeper who has seen your spending habits and has opinions but keeps them to two-line comments in the margin. Nothing here is a "stat" or a "graph." Everything is a posted entry, a running balance, a budget line, or a printed receipt, and the emotional payoff is the accountant's small daily satisfaction of closing the books and watching them balance to zero.
WHY: Double-entry bookkeeping is the rare metaphor that is not decorative here, because the underlying data genuinely is directional and paired. Network activity is literally bytes sent against bytes received, disk activity is literally bytes written against bytes read, and AI usage is literally tokens you prompted against tokens the model generated, so each of the three has a natural debit side and credit side that maps onto ledger columns without being forced. Screen time and input are the two families that do not naturally pair, and the accounting frame handles that honestly by booking them as expense accounts rather than pretending they balance, which is exactly how a real ledger treats overhead you cannot net against revenue. The frame also gives real meaning to aggregation, the founder's central goal, because a ledger's whole purpose is to roll many small heterogeneous transactions up into a few account totals and a single closing figure, which is precisely the "one dashboard of digital life" he asked for. Finally, accounting is the one domain whose entire professional ethic is auditability and provenance, which turns the app's privacy obligation into a native virtue rather than a disclaimer.
TONE: The voice is a competent, faintly weary bookkeeper with dry wit and zero moralizing. It states figures plainly, notes anomalies in a flat register ("Network receipts up 340% over trailing average. No judgment. Filing it."), and reserves warmth for the moment the books balance. It never nags, never gamifies with confetti or stre0aks-as-guilt, and never editorializes about whether your numbers are "good." Its humor comes from applying grave financial seriousness to trivial digital minutiae, treating a spike in mouse travel with the same sober diligence as a material accounting event. The tone must stay honest and legible above all, because the entire brand promise is that this book can be trusted.
VISUAL: The look is a modern accountant's desk, not a skeuomorphic pastiche. Type is the load-bearing element: figures and ledger rows are set in IBM Plex Mono (tabular numerals, so columns align to the pixel), account names and running headers in Berkeley Mono or, as a system-safe fallback, SF Mono, and the rare piece of prose in the receipts is set in a condensed grotesque, ideally Söhne or the free Inter Tight, to read as printed footer copy. The palette is ledger paper and ink. The base surface is a warm off-white paper tone (#F4F1E9) in light mode and a deep ink-navy (#141A24) in dark mode. Debits post in a desaturated oxblood red (#9B3B2F), credits in a muted ledger green (#3B6B4A), running balances in near-black ink (#1C1C1A), and every account is separated by hairline rules in a faded pencil gray (#C9C2B2). A single accent, a brass-ledger gold (#B58A3C), marks the "books balanced" state and nothing else, so gold always means resolution. Motion is deliberately mechanical and sparse: numbers roll up on a split-flap-style tick when an entry posts, the nightly receipt feeds downward line by line as if fed through a platen, and reconciliation ends with a faint rubber-stamp thunk and a "POSTED" impression. Texture is limited to a barely-there paper grain on receipts and a subtle perforation edge, never a photographic printer bezel. The overall discipline is restraint, because an accountant's ledger earns trust by looking plain and exact.
FORM: The primary residence is the menubar extra, which shows a single glyph plus today's running balance figure, and whose dropdown is a compact live "day sheet" listing the five accounts with their current debit and credit totals and a Reconcile button pinned at the bottom. The main dashboard window is the General Ledger, a full-height document window laid out like a bound accounting book with a left rail of accounts, a central column of posted entries for the selected period, and a right rail holding the trial balance and budget lines. The signature secondary surface is the nightly receipt, which renders as a tall narrow thermal-printer strip that can live as its own always-on-top mini window, be saved to disk, or feed into a desktop widget. Desktop widgets come in two sizes: a small "Today's Balance" chit and a medium "Open Accounts" card showing the five account totals as a mini trial balance. A screensaver ships as the long-run flourish, an endless scroll of the day's receipt tape unspooling down a dark screen, but it is explicitly a version-1.1 stretch rather than a launch requirement.
VOCAB:
- AI usage (tokens consumed and generated) -> The Token Account, split into Tokens Payable (prompted out) and Tokens Receivable (generated back) | A two-column ledger account where each session posts as a dated entry, prompt tokens booked as a debit and completion tokens as a credit, with a running balance and a small 'exchange rate' footnote showing your generated-to-prompted ratio. When only Claude Code local logs are available, the account header reads 'Partial: 1 of N sources reporting' and unbooked tools appear as grayed 'accounts not yet opened,' so the missing data is disclosed rather than hidden.
- Network activity (bytes sent and received) -> Traffic Account, as Bytes Remitted (sent) against Bytes Received | The cleanest true double-entry line in the book, shown as paired debit and credit columns that tally to a net flow figure, with a sparkline-free running balance because the accountant does not do charts, only totals and deltas against the trailing seven-period average.
- Disk activity (bytes read and written) -> Storage Account, as Writes Posted against Reads Drawn | Booked identically to Traffic with writes as debits and reads as credits, plus a single derived 'churn' line that reports the day's total posted volume, so heavy build or indexing days read as large but balanced entries rather than alarms.
- Screen time (time before an awake display) -> an expense account titled Hours Under the Lamp | Because time does not pair against a byte credit, it is booked honestly as an overhead expense in HH:MM rather than forced into balance, shown as a single debit line accruing through the day against an optional daily budget, with the budget bar the only non-monospace-figure visual and even that rendered as a plain filled rule.
- Input (keystrokes and mouse travel) -> the Labor Account, split into Keys Struck (count) and Distance Hauled (cumulative mouse travel in meters) | Two expense sub-lines under one account, keystrokes as an integer count and mouse travel converted to human-legible meters or kilometers with a deadpan unit ('1.2 km hauled'), both accruing as debits with no credit side, framed explicitly as the manual labor cost of running the estate.
SIGNATURE: The signature feature is Reconciliation, the nightly closing ritual. At end of day the user clicks Reconcile, and the app runs a real closing procedure: it freezes the period, tallies every account, prints the day's receipt line by line down a thermal strip, and ends by stamping the books either BALANCED in brass gold or, when a data source dropped out mid-day, FLAGGED with a plain note of exactly which account is short and why. The receipt is a genuine artifact with a header (date, period, machine name), an itemized body of the five accounts with debits and credits, a totals block, one dry margin comment generated from the day's standout figure, and a footer bearing a content hash of the underlying local data so the entry is tamper-evident. Reconciled days become immutable and get bound into the ledger's back-book, which means the app accrues a browsable, auditable financial history of your digital life that you can flip through like old statements, and the daily two-second stamp becomes the satisfying habit loop the whole product is built around.
RITUALS: The daily ritual is the nightly Reconcile-and-stamp described above, a deliberate two-second close that swaps the open, mutable day for a bound, immutable receipt. The weekly ritual is the Statement, issued each Sunday night as a longer receipt that carries forward the seven daily closes into weekly account totals, variances against the prior week, and any budget lines that ran over, written in the same flat bookkeeper's register. The yearly ritual is the Annual Report, a bound multi-page document delivered on the account's anniversary and at year-end that reads like a tiny company's filing: opening and closing balances for each account, the year's largest single entries, the ratio of tokens receivable to payable, total hours under the lamp, and total distance hauled, closing with one page of dry executive commentary. Each cadence uses the identical receipt grammar at a larger denomination, so the rituals feel like the same practice deepening rather than three different features.
PRIVACY: The stance is stated in accounting terms and is therefore both honest and on-brand: this ledger records amounts, never contents, and the books never leave the building. Concretely, everything is computed and stored locally in a single on-device ledger file, no entry ever carries the substance of a transaction (the Token Account knows a session cost 4,200 prompt tokens but never a word of the prompt, the Labor Account counts that 8,000 keys were struck but never which keys, and the Traffic Account books byte volumes without payloads, destinations, or URLs), and there is no account, no telemetry, and no network egress originating from the app itself. The app leans into audit as a feature rather than a liability: the user can open the raw ledger file and the receipt hashes and verify for themselves that only counts are held, and the founder's honest confidence comes from the fact that there is nothing sensitive to leak because the sensitive layer was never recorded in the first place.
V1 CUTS: Version 1 deliberately omits several tempting features to keep the books trustworthy and the scope native. It ships no cloud sync, no companion iOS app, and no cross-device consolidation, because a second building would break the local-only promise that anchors the brand. It excludes goal-based gamification, social sharing, and leaderboards outright, since guilt-driven streaks contradict the no-judgment voice. It does not attempt to auto-detect every AI tool at launch and instead supports Claude Code local logs first with a clearly disclosed 'accounts not yet opened' state for the rest, rather than shipping unreliable scraping that would poison the audit trail. It leaves out predictive analytics and forecasting, because an accountant reports what posted, not what might. The screensaver and any charting beyond simple filled budget rules are pushed to later versions, so version 1 stays a lean, exact, honest book rather than a dashboard wearing a ledger costume.
RISK: The biggest risk is that the accounting metaphor is intellectually elegant but emotionally cold, and that users who came for a friendly quantified-self dashboard bounce off a product that speaks in debits and refuses to tell them whether their numbers are good. The double-entry conceit also strains where the data does not pair, since screen time and input have no credit side and are only honestly bookable as expenses, so if the app oversells 'everything balances' it will feel like a gimmick the moment a curious user notices two of five accounts never balance to zero. The mitigation is to let the metaphor bend rather than break by booking unpaired families frankly as overhead, to earn the coldness back through the genuine satisfaction of the nightly stamp and the browsable bound history, and to make the dry wit warm enough at the moment of reconciliation that the accountant reads as a trusted confidant rather than an indifferent machine.
